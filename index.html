What is EarthSurveyor?
EarthSurveyor is a standalone application that allows students to explore the world on various levels. It is designed as a tool for Geography students in high school. There are multiple layers of information available and the application can be used in a laboratory-like setting. Sample questions are available here but it is adaptable as well.
Source Data - The App includes these layers
  • Major Cities - All Cities over 100,000 people - map data
  • Regions of the world - map data
  • Vegetation Types - map data
  • World Wildlife Fund Terrestrial Ecoregions - map data
  • Population Density - map data
  • Global Land Cover Land Use - map data
  • Topographic (Basemap) - map data

    Sample Questions for the High School Classroom
  • What is the most common land use in Western Australia?
  • How many cities larger than 1,000,000 are there in Nigeria?
  • What is the most common type of vegetation in the Carribbean?
  • In which region is this location: 1.06N, 113.52E?

  • In South America what type of terrestrial ecoregion is most present south of the Tropic of Capricorn?
  • Which physical feature dominates the landscape of Namibia?
  • The states of Tajikistan and Kyrgyzstan are at mostly what two types of climate? Breifly describe these climates. What physical feature contributes to this?

  • Find these three locations: (25.15N, 105.64W);(39.24N, 72.94E);(23.78S, 45.66W). At which of those places was this photo taken?
